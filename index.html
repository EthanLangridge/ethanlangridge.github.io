About me

I will be a first-year Mechanical Engineering student at the University of Hertfordshire, with a strong interest in fluid dynamics and biomimetic engineering. During college I completed a CAMTEC Extended Certificate in Engineering along with A levels in Physics and Mathematics, developing skills in CAD, CFD, sustainability analysis, and technical report writing.

My current research focus is on riblet drag reduction — a passive aerodynamic surface treatment inspired by shark skin microstructure. I am exploring its application to small UAVs and automotive platforms, particularly at the low Reynolds numbers relevant to those systems.

Outside of academics I will be joining the university's Formula Student team and maintain a personal research log documenting my progress in this area.

Focus areas

Research interests

01 — Aerodynamics

Riblet drag reduction

Investigating how V-groove riblet geometry — inspired by the dermal denticles of fast-swimming sharks — reduces turbulent skin friction drag. Focus on low Reynolds number regimes relevant to small UAVs, where existing literature is limited.

Biomimicry
02 — Application

UAV surface optimisation

Exploring passive drag reduction techniques on small unmanned aerial vehicles, where endurance is severely limited by aerodynamic inefficiency. Zero-power solutions are particularly valuable in defence contexts.

UAV / Defence
03 — Motorsport

Wheel arch aerodynamics

Investigating passive and active flow management to reduce aerodynamic losses associated with rotating wheels in wheel arches — an underexplored source of drag on road and competition vehicles.

Formula Student
04 — Methods

Computational fluid dynamics

Developing CFD skills to model boundary layer behaviour over textured surfaces. Computational validation will precede physical wind tunnel testing of riblet geometries.
